US-01 · Timeline Awareness

When should I start?

The biggest mistake CIS students make is treating the internship search as a senior-year task. Most competitive roles close months before the semester starts. Here's what the hiring calendar actually looks like.

1
Sophomore Year · Spring
Start building your professional presence
Set up LinkedIn, attend CIS RSO events through PIN, and connect with upperclassmen. You're not applying yet — you're building the network that will refer you later.
2
Junior Year · Fall (August–October)
Large-company recruiting windows open
Big tech, consulting, and Fortune 500 firms post summer internships in August–October for the following year. If you wait until spring, these pipelines are already closed.
⚠ Apply to large companies NOW — not in spring
3
Junior Year · Spring (January–March)
Mid-size and startup roles open up
Smaller companies and startups recruit closer to the semester. This is your second wave — and often where CIS-specific roles (cybersecurity, IT, dev) appear more frequently.
4
Before Your Internship Starts
Apply for CIS 4970 BEFORE day one
You must have your position reviewed and approved before registering for CIS 4970. Retroactive credit is not granted. Submit your Handshake application as soon as you accept an offer.
🚨 Do NOT start the internship before applying for CIS 4970
5
Fall 2026 · August 28 at 5 PM
Absolute final deadline
All applications must be submitted by the last day of late registration. No exceptions are made for submissions after 5 PM. No holidays are processed during May 25, June 19, or July 3.
US-02 · Qualifying Internship Clarity

Does my role count?

Not every internship qualifies for CIS 4970 credit. Use this guide to evaluate a posting before you invest time in the application — and understand what the approval process looks like once you land it.

✓ Qualifies
✅ CIS-related duties (IT, cybersecurity, dev, data, systems)
✅ At least 20 hours per week
✅ Internship, co-op, part-time, or full-time job
✅ Position dates overlap Aug 31 – Dec 4 (for Fall 2026)
✅ New position only (not a past internship)
✗ Does Not Qualify
❌ Help desk or Tier 1 support roles
❌ Fewer than 20 hours per week
❌ Non-CIS-related work (retail, admin, unrelated fields)
❌ Past or retroactive internships
❌ Positions with no overlap with the academic term

The Approval Process — Step by Step

1
Accept your offer and gather proof of employment (offer letter or redacted pay stub).
2
Log into Handshake at gsu.joinhandshake.com → Career Center → Experiences → Submit an Experience.
3
Select template: "CIS 4970 Field Study in CIS Capstone Application" and choose your term.
4
Complete all sections: student info, position details, company info, field study outline and objectives (5–10 objectives required), and field study source.
5
Attach proof of employment and click Submit an experience. You'll receive a confirmation email.
6
International students needing CPT: Contact Dr. Napier at nnapier1@gsu.edu once registered. CPT approval takes ~2 weeks.
US-03 · Application Readiness

Am I ready to apply?

The goal is to be fully application-ready before a single position opens — so when you see a role, you apply within hours, not days. Use this checklist to get there.

Resume & Materials

Resume is tailored to CIS roles: includes technical skills, relevant coursework, and any projects or certifications (e.g., Security+, Google IT)
Resume reviewed by GSU Career Services or a peer — grammar, formatting, and bullet strength confirmed
Cover letter template drafted with 2–3 interchangeable paragraphs you can customize per role in under 15 minutes
LinkedIn profile complete with photo, summary, skills, and all coursework or projects listed

CIS 4970 Prerequisites

CIS 3260 (Intro to Programming) completed with a B or better
9 additional credit hours of 3000- or 4000-level CIS courses completed or in progress
45 semester hours total completed (RCB upper division course requirement)

Before Applying for CIS 4970

Proof of employment ready to attach (offer letter or redacted pay stub)
CIS 4970 Field Study Handbook reviewed — responses drafted in MS Word before copying into Handshake
Field Study Outline & Objectives written: 5–10 full-sentence objectives with specific milestones ready
US-04 · Job Search Strategy

Where and how to search

A curated list of platforms and outreach strategies vetted for CIS roles — so you focus your time on approaches that actually lead to callbacks, not into a black hole.

Handshake
Top Pick

GSU's primary recruiting platform. Employers posting here are actively seeking GSU students. Filter by CIS or IS concentration.

LinkedIn Jobs
Top Pick

Filter by "internship" + your CIS concentration keyword. Easy Apply is fast but write a targeted note when you can connect with a recruiter.

Indeed
Broad Reach

High volume of postings. Search terms: "cybersecurity intern," "IT analyst intern," "data analyst intern Atlanta." Sort by date, not relevance.

WayUp / Chegg
Student-Focused

Platforms built for students with verified internship postings. Good for finding roles from smaller companies actively seeking undergrads.

GSU Career Fair
High ROI

In-person or virtual fairs give you face time with recruiters. Follow up via LinkedIn within 24 hours — name recognition matters significantly.

CIS RSO Network
Network

Referrals from alumni and upperclassmen in GSU CIS orgs convert at much higher rates than cold applications. Join RSOs early through PIN (pin.gsu.edu).

Outreach Strategies That Work

Apply within the first 48 hours of a posting going live — application review often begins immediately, and early applicants get more attention.
After applying, find the recruiter or hiring manager on LinkedIn and send a brief connection note referencing the role. This doubles your visibility.
Tailor your resume for each role's keywords — many companies use ATS (applicant tracking systems) that filter before a human ever sees your application.
Track every application in a spreadsheet: company, role, date applied, status, follow-up date. Without this, opportunities fall through the cracks.
For cybersecurity-specific roles, highlight certifications (Security+, CompTIA, Google) prominently. These differentiate you significantly from general CIS applicants.
